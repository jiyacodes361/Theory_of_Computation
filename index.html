Important Questions (CO 1)

1. Prove by mathematical induction: 1 + 3 + 5 + … + (2n − 1) = n²
2. Define reflexivity, symmetry and transitivity of relations.
3. Check whether relation R={(1,2),(1,1),(2,1),(2,2),(3,2),(3,3)} is reflexive, symmetric and transitive.
4. Write principle of mathematical induction.
5. Prove: sum of squares formula.
6. Explain one-to-one, onto and bijection with example.
7. Explain equivalence relation with example.
8. Define onto and one-to-one function.
9. Recursive definition of tree.
10. Truth table for P → (¬P ∨ ¬Q).
11. Proof by contradiction: if A∩B=∅ and C⊆B then A∩C=∅.
12. Induction proof of Σ 1/(i(i+1)).
13. Define relation and prove reflexive but not transitive relation.
14. Prove Bernoulli inequality (1-a)^n ≥ 1−na.
15. Prove √3 is irrational.
16. Composition and inverse of functions.
17. Identify tautology / contradiction statements.
18. Prove every string of length 4 over {a,b} contains substring xx.
19. Represent infinite sets using { | n∈N } notation.
20. Quantifier truth statements over interval (0,1) and [0,1].
21. If A×B ⊆ B×A prove A=B.
22. If f:A→B is one-to-one or onto determine size of B.
23. Check if function f(a,b)=(a+b,a−b) is one-to-one and onto.
24. Prove properties of function over union/intersection of sets.
25. Determine reflexive, symmetric, transitive properties for relations.
26. Mathematical induction proofs (various).
27. Prove √2 irrational.
28. Rational + irrational number property.
29. Sum of positive numbers property.
30. Sum of two even numbers property.
31. Negative of irrational number property.
